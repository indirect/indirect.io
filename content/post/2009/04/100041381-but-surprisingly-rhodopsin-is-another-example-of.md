Once in Archaea and once in Eubacteria.<br/>This fact should shock us. The number of possible proteins is astronomical. There is an alphabet of 20 base symbols (amino acids), which make up every protein &quot;word&rdquo; which on average is say, 100 symbols, or 100 bases, long. Because only one in a million &ldquo;words&rdquo; fold into a functioning protein, let&rsquo;s vastly reduce that magnitude and agree that the number of potential working proteins is equal to the number of stars in the universe.
